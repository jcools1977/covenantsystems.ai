🎯 QUALIFICATION CRITERIA (Who to Say Yes To)

GREEN LIGHT (High-Probability Fit)

  • Solo or small firm (1-10 attorneys)
  • High-volume practice (family law, immigration, estate planning, personal injury)
  • Uses existing practice management software (Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther)
  • Spending 10+ hours/week on manual administrative work
  • Decision-maker on the call (partner, owner)
  • Budget authority (not "I need to check with my partner")

YELLOW LIGHT (Possible Fit, Needs Assessment)

  • Firm size 11-20 attorneys (might need enterprise solution instead)
  • Low-volume/boutique practice (may not have enough repetitive work to automate)
  • No current practice management software (will need to set up infrastructure first)
  • Contact is office manager or paralegal, not attorney (may lack budget authority)

RED LIGHT (Not a Fit, Politely Decline)

  • BigLaw (20+ attorneys) - they need enterprise vendors, not us
  • Startup attorney with 0-2 clients - not enough volume to justify automation cost
  • Looking for free/DIY solution - not our market
  • "Can you build us a custom practice management system?" - out of scope
  • Wants hourly billing instead of fixed fee - not our model

📞 DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT

Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for taking the time today, [Name]. I know you're busy, so I'll keep this focused. The goal of this call is to understand your current workflows and see if automation makes sense for your practice. I'll be honest if I don't think it's a fit — I'd rather tell you now than waste your time. Sound good?"

Qualification Questions (10 min)

  1. Firm overview: "Tell me about your practice — how many attorneys, what practice areas?"
  2. Current tools: "What practice management software are you using? Clio, MyCase, something else?"
  3. Pain points: "What's eating up the most non-billable time right now? Document drafting, client intake, scheduling, something else?"
  4. Volume: "How many new clients do you onboard per month? How many [documents/contracts/letters] are you drafting per week?"
  5. Current process: "Walk me through what happens when a new client reaches out. What are all the manual steps?"

Solution Positioning (8 min)

"Based on what you've described, here's what I'd recommend automating..."

  • Describe 2-3 specific workflows tailored to their pain points
  • Explain how it integrates with their existing tools
  • Give time savings estimate: "This would save you roughly [X] hours per week"
  • Mention pricing range: "For a firm your size, this would be in the $[X]-$[Y] range"

Objection Handling

"That sounds expensive."
"I get it. Let's do the math: you said you're spending 10 hours a week on [task]. At your billable rate of $[X]/hour, that's $[Y] per month in lost revenue. Our investment pays for itself in [Z] weeks."

"Can I just use Zapier myself?"
"Absolutely, if you have the time to learn it and maintain it. Most attorneys we work with would rather spend that time on cases. We also handle the integrations, testing, and troubleshooting. But if you want to DIY, I can point you to some resources."

"I need to think about it / talk to my partner."
"Totally fair. What questions do you have that would help you make the decision? I can send a written proposal so you have everything in writing to review."

Closing (5 min)

"So here's what happens next: I'll send you a written proposal by [date]. It'll include the specific workflows we discussed, timeline, pricing, and what's included. You review it, and if it makes sense, we can kick off next week. Any questions before I send that over?"

🚨 ESCALATION PATHS

When to Escalate to DeVere

  • Client wants custom legal terms beyond our standard agreement
  • Client asking for equity/rev-share instead of cash payment
  • Pricing request outside standard range (e.g., $10K+ project)
  • Technical requirements beyond current capability (e.g., "build us a custom AI model")
  • Client requests in-person meeting or on-site work
  • Legal/liability questions (e.g., "Are you responsible if the workflow has a bug?")

When to Escalate to Technical Lead (ABED)

  • Client uses obscure practice management software we haven't integrated before
  • Workflow requirements involve API limitations or rate limits
  • Client wants HIPAA compliance or specific security certifications
  • AI model requests that require fine-tuning or custom training
Default Answer for Unknowns: "That's a great question. Let me check with our technical team and get back to you within 24 hours." Better to promise less and over-deliver than to commit to something we can't do.
