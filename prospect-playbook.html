Top 5 Prospect Questions & Response Templates

QUESTION 1: "What's your security posture? Do you have SOC 2?"

Response (Email/Form Follow-up):

Thanks for your interest in Covenant Systems. Great question on security. We are a pre-SOC 2 company — SOC 2 is a growth-stage investment we're planning for later this year. However, we have comprehensive security controls in place today: • AES-256 encryption for all data in transit and at rest • TLS 1.3 for all API connections • AWS infrastructure with industry-standard access controls • 90-day retention for briefing data; 24-month retention for derived intelligence patterns • Documented Written Information Security Program (WISP) available for your review • We do NOT store client data — intelligence only, no integration needed Most CPA firms in the pilot phase accept our current security posture. SOC 2 becomes important when you integrate with your core systems — which we don't require. For your specific security requirements, I've attached our WISP, Privacy Policy, and Terms of Service. Happy to discuss any specific controls your firm requires.

Documents to Attach:

  • WISP v2 (Written Information Security Program)
  • Privacy Policy
  • Terms of Service
  • Vendor Due Diligence Responses (if they send a vendor form)

Next Step: Schedule a 15-minute call to discuss their specific requirements.

QUESTION 2: "How is this different from Artifact AI / [Competitor]?"

Response (Email/Phone):

Great question. Here's the difference: OUR APPROACH: We deliver a daily intelligence briefing to your inbox. No new software. No dashboards. No logins. We read the regulatory sources, industry publications, and market data that matter to your firm — and send you a concise summary every morning. You decide what to do with the intelligence. COMPETITOR APPROACH: They typically automate workflows or help you do existing tasks faster (e.g., automate tax return prep, speed up bookkeeping). They require software integration and usually pull data from your systems. WHY IT MATTERS: You're drowning in information, not in workflow steps. The intelligence you need exists — it's just scattered across 100 different sources. We're the daily digest. They're the efficiency layer. Most CPA firms benefit from BOTH, but you should start with intelligence because it doesn't require deployment risk.

Talking Points (if on phone):

  • Ask what their biggest intelligence challenge is
  • Listen for: "Keeping up with tax changes," "Knowing what clients will ask about," "Industry trends"
  • Mirror back: "That's exactly why we built this — to answer those questions before you're in the middle of a client conversation"

QUESTION 3: "Do you need access to our QuickBooks / Tax software / Client data?"

Response (Email/Phone):

No. Not at all. That's actually a core design principle for us. We deliver business intelligence — regulatory updates, tax law changes, industry trends, market data. We don't need integration with your core systems. No API keys. No data exports. No risk. This is why we're a good first-step AI investment for your firm. You get the intelligence benefit with zero deployment risk.

Why This Matters: CPAs are nervous about data access. This is your biggest trust-building line.

Next Step: If they're convinced this is low-risk, ask: "Would you be interested in a small pilot?"

Response SLA & Escalation

Response Timeline

  • Phone call: Answer same day (or call back within 4 hours)
  • Email/Form: Respond within 24 hours with answer + next steps
  • Multiple follow-ups: Each response within 24 hours

Escalation Logic

Who responds?

Form submission (hello@): DeVere OR LUKE sees it first. Route appropriately:

  • Security question → Send SOL's response templates
  • Pricing/Pilot question → DeVere decides on commitment level
  • Time-sensitive (they give deadline) → Escalate to DeVere immediately

Phone call: DeVere or ELIAS answers

  • If it's a security question: Answer using templates, offer call follow-up
  • If it's a "close the deal" call: Get commitment to Design Partner Agreement

Design Partner Agreement Path

Once a prospect expresses interest in a pilot:

Step 1: Send Design Partner Agreement

Cover message: "We'd like to move forward with a small pilot. Here's the agreement that outlines the arrangement."

Step 2: Review Loop

Prospect reviews agreement → raises questions (likely: data handling, timeline, what they need to do) → respond with clarity → SOL reviews any edits before signature

Step 3: Execution

Prospect signs → we counter-sign → pilot begins

Step 4: Launch Intelligence Briefings

Set up daily briefing delivery → first briefing within 24 hours → collect feedback every 2 weeks

Critical Reminders

DO:

  • Respond fast (within 24 hours)
  • Use the response templates (for consistency)
  • Send documents proactively (security, privacy, terms)
  • Ask about their pain points (so we learn)
  • Offer a pilot, not a commitment (lower friction)
  • Copy SOL on any legal questions
  • Escalate to DeVere if they want to close a deal

DON'T:

  • Overclaim what we do ("AI that reads everything")
  • Promise pricing or timeline we haven't committed to
  • Suggest we need access to their data
  • Use filler language or buzzwords
  • Delay response (24 hours is the SLA)
  • Make unilateral decisions about pilot commitment
